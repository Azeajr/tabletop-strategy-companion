## Axis A — Strategic optimality

Connect-4 is a **SOLVED game** (Allis 1988 PhD thesis, confirmed by modern solver databases). The seed correctly sources and prioritizes the established optimal lines:

1. **First-mover opening (S0)** — Column 4 (center) is the ONLY winning first move for P1; columns 3/5 draw; columns 1/2/6/7 lose. Source: solver databases verified against Allis. ✓

2. **Second-mover reply (S1)** — P2 draws with perfect play by stacking column 4 or playing adjacent (3 or 5); all other replies lose. Correct ceiling. ✓

3. **Threat building (S2)** — Horizontal + diagonal from a shared disc, two playable extensions = forced win. Correct principle. ✓

4. **Claim-even parity strategy (S3, S9)** — P2's path to draw is occupying even-numbered rows (rows 2, 4, 6 from bottom); each move advances an even-row threat or blocks P1's odd-row threat. Correct. ✓

5. **Forcing move chains (S4)** — Chaining forced responses in different areas leads to zugzwang and double threats. Correct. ✓

6. **Odd-even parity in zugzwang (S5)** — On a full board, P1 wins threats on odd rows (1, 3, 5); P2 wins threats on even rows (2, 4, 6). Correct principle, though the framing ("P1: odd rows (1,3,5) win threats") is slightly abstract without the full context that this rule applies in ultra-endgame racing. The TLDR ("Late-game parity decides the race") is sound. ✓

7. **Zugzwang avoidance (S6)** — Don't place a disc that creates a winning position for the opponent one row above. Correct defensive principle. ✓

8. **Double threat closure (S7)** — A double threat (one disc completing two different four-in-a-rows) guarantees a win. Correct. ✓

9. **Endgame forced win (S8)** — Calculate 3 moves deep and verify no blocking alternative. Correct guidance. ✓

10. **Endgame drawing defense (S9)** — P2 applies claim-even in the endgame to force P1's threats onto wrong-parity rows. Correct. ✓
