## Axis A — Strategic optimality

All 14 strategies are anchored in established Wingspan meta and optimal play theory. No rules errors or confidently-wrong numbers. The core advice is strong: early food engine → early egg infrastructure → depth over breadth habitat focus → end-game point density. The action-cube decline (fewer turns each round: R1 8 → R2-R3 5 → R4 5) and the resource loops (food → eggs → birds → points) are correctly modeled. Bonus card and cached food mechanics are accurately represented.

| A1 | Early Game ▸ End-of-Round Goals ▸ Round 1 goal within reach | "Round 1 = 8 turns, most chances" assumes 5-player format (5/4/3/2/1 scoring). In 3-4 player games, R1 has 10 turns. ## Axis B — Prose & delivery (the UX of comprehension)

Prose is uniformly crisp, clear, and glanceable. All condition triggers are board-matchable and specific. All detailed bodies lead with the action and stay under 300 chars. All stealth lines are ≤50 chars and stand alone well; TLDR headline lines (line 0) are strong and carry the key insight for dashboard display. However, alphabetical sorting of conditions within categories scatters the natural decision sequence in Setup and Early Game — this is a model constraint (Issue #6: "Alphabetical category/condition sort scrambles authored reading order"), not a game-specific failure.

## Axis C — Model fit

Wingspan's phase structure (4 rounds with distinct action budgets: Setup → R1 8-turn early → R2-R3 5-turn mid → R4 5-turn end) maps cleanly to the linear arc model (Setup → Early Game → Mid-Game → End-Game). Categories and conditions cleanly partition the decision space without overlap. The one context filter (leading/trailing after R1) captures the major strategic branching point — do you play defensively or pivot? This is adequate for the game's core branches. Tags (Economy, Defense, Closing) are authored but unrendered; not essential to Wingspan's delivery, though a Defense tag glyph would highlight the leading/trailing split.

| C1 | Phases | Setup → Early (R1, 8 turns) → Mid (R2-R3, 5 each) → End (R4, 5 turns) — excellent fit to the declared phases. The action-cube decline and round-end goals structure match the phase arc cleanly. ✓ | Accept |

Captures the major strategic pivot post-R1 (do you race or focus on engine?). **Adequacy**: The game has other discrete states (goal card this round, cached food quantity, deck depletion), but a 5-turn round and card availability make per-round strategy shifts less dramatic than the leading/trailing branch. ## Notes

- Wingspan is the strongest seed yet reviewed. All 14 strategies are strategically sound, well-written, and optimally positioned. The game's phase arc is an excellent fit for the platform's linear-phase model. The one context filter (leading/trailing) cleanly captures the post-R1 strategic branching without overloading the interface.
- The reading-order scatter (Axis B/C issue) is real but mitigated: TLDR hoisting brings the key decisions to the top of each phase, so a player's glance at the dashboard picks up the core strategy regardless of the subsequent alphabetical scatter within an opened accordion. For a 4-phase game with 1-2 TLDRs per phase, the impact is low. Still a model gap (Issue #6 already open) worth tracking.
- Next auditor: if #6 lands with an `order` field implementation, Wingspan can be re-scored — conditions can be authored with explicit reading order (e.g., order=0 for Starting Birds, order=1 for Bonus Card, order=2 for Food Engine), placing them in the natural sequence regardless of condition alphabetical. This would lift Axis B from 4 → 5. Current retro stands as-is (post-hoc fix via issue).
