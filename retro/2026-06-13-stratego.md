## Axis A — Strategic optimality

The offensive and defensive theory is sound and grounded in competitive Stratego:

- **Bomb placement & flag protection** (Setup): Corner + 2–3 adjacent bombs is the standard high-risk trap; the prior pass already tightened the line to include "1 row up, bombs behind = surprise" as a deceptive alternate. Both are correct.
- **Miner economy** (Mid-Game, End-Game): The seed correctly identifies that you have only 3 Miners per side and treats them as the endgame winning pieces. The mid-game "route through protected corridor" and the end-game bait-and-flank tactics are established optimal lines.
- **Spy-vs-Marshal** (Mid-Game, context="marshal-hidden"): The critical rule — "Spy beats Marshal ONLY on attack" — is correctly stated. If Marshal moves first, Spy loses. This is the game's most important single-piece matchup and the condition correctly gates it to the hidden-Marshal phase.
- **Scout (rank 2) info-gathering** (Setup): Correct that Scouts are the only rank-2 pieces that beat nothing; any rank 3+ kills them. Clustering warning is strategically sound.
- **Bluffing & deception** (Early Game via context filter): Marshal Concealment ("keep Marshal stationary") vs Marshal Pressure ("use Marshal as known threat") is the right split and reflects the actual strategic fork.
