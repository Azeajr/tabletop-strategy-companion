## Axis A — Strategic optimality

The core meta is strong and correctly sourced: **orange/red highest landing frequency** (jail proximity post-release), **3-house peak ROI** (scales 6→18→30 rent per $150 build, then 45→60→80 with diminishing marginal return), **housing shortage squeeze** (only 32 tokens in the bank), **jail dynamics** (early = leave, late = collect safely from rim), and **trading-first strategy** (monopolies unlock exponential rent). These are the established lines (Parker Bros. math, decades of play data).

One execution issue on hotel mechanics: **S6 detailed says "Hotels return 4 houses to the bank, ending your shortage weapon."** The phrasing is technically accurate (you surrender 4 houses when you convert the hotel) but the word "return" suggests refund/restoration rather than the 1:1 trade it actually is — a player might misread this as "hotels give you an extra 4 houses." Stealth line was clarified in this pass (see Axis B).

## Axis C — Model fit

Monopoly's decision structure maps cleanly to the Phase → Category → Condition → Strategy arc:
- **Phases**: Setup → Early Game → Mid-Game → End-Game is the canonical Monopoly progression. ✓
- **Categories**: meaningful and roughly MECE (Property Priority, Railroads, Trading, Jail, Building, Cash Mgmt, Liquidation, Survival). ✓
- **Conditions**: partition the decision space well (when to buy, when to trade, when to jail, when to mortgage). ✓

**Correction (post-audit review).** An earlier draft of this retro claimed the cash-rich/poor filter was "sticky — set once at session start, not re-evaluable mid-game." That is wrong. `InlineYesNoFilter` is a live yes / no / clear toggle the player can re-tap any turn; `filter1` is a reactive signal and `phaseStrategies` recomputes immediately. Cash position being dynamic is a non-issue — the player just re-taps when it flips. There is no model gap here.
